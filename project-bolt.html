The Problem

When a 10-minute ride became a 26-minute crisis

While rushing to catch a train in Erlangen with my wife, I rented two Bolt scooters for what should have been a simple 10-minute ride. Instead, we experienced cascading failures across digital and physical product layers that turned a quick trip into a stressful ordeal.

"What should have cost €4 and 10 minutes became €16 and nearly missing our train entirely."

3 Critical Gaps

No Proactive Warnings

App had parking data but never warned me before I started

No Safe Navigation

Had to hold phone illegally or stop constantly to check directions

Unsafe Turn Signals

Hand signals = removing hand from handlebar = nearly falling

UX Audit: Current State Analysis

I analyzed Bolt's existing app and competitors (Voi, Lime, Tier) to identify systematic UX gaps

Heuristic Evaluation Results

❌ Error Prevention

Score: 2/10

No warnings before users start rides to parking-limited destinations. System knows parking is full but doesn't communicate this proactively.

❌ Visibility of System Status

Score: 3/10

Users can't see parking availability in real-time during navigation. Must stop and manually check app while riding.

❌ Match Between System & Real World

Score: 4/10

App forces context switching to Maps for navigation. Users must hold phone illegally or stop frequently—doesn't match real riding behavior.
