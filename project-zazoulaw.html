02 — Research

Understanding User Needs

Research Note: This is a conceptual design project based on competitive analysis of 20+ legal platforms (Egyptian law firms, international legal tech apps), secondary research on digital service adoption in Egypt, and insights from informal conversations with legal professionals. User statistics are derived from industry reports and market research studies on digital booking preferences in professional services. No formal user interviews or usability testing were conducted.

Through competitive analysis and secondary research on Egyptian legal service usage patterns, I identified key pain points and created personas representing different user segments. These insights shaped the app's core features and user experience strategy.

03 — Design Process

From Insights to Interface

Design Goal: Make legal services as accessible and transparent as ordering food delivery.

Design Principles

Arabic-First Design

Not just translation—native RTL experience. Every screen, interaction, and flow designed for Arabic reading patterns.

Trust Through Transparency

Upfront pricing, visible credentials, real-time availability. No hidden information.

Professional Yet Approachable

Navy blue (trust) + gold (premium) balanced with clean, modern UI to reduce intimidation.

Mobile-First

Thumb-friendly design, quick actions, on-the-go accessibility.

Arabic-First RTL Design

Beyond mirroring—native Arabic experience:

  • Natural text flow: Reading patterns from right to left
  • Icon placement: Arrows, chevrons, and navigation adjusted
  • Cultural context: Using appropriate imagery and metaphors
  • Font selection: Cairo font for optimal Arabic readability

Building Trust

Every design element reinforces credibility:

  • Lawyer credentials prominent: Education, years of experience, specializations
  • Rating system visible: Star ratings and success rates
  • Status indicators: Green (available), Yellow (busy), Gray (offline)
  • Verified badges: Crown icon for premium lawyers

Accessibility & Usability

  • Thumb-friendly design: Navigation bar at bottom, key actions within reach
  • Clear CTAs: Gold buttons stand out against navy backgrounds
  • Feedback at every step: Loading states, confirmations, error messages
  • Both light & dark modes: Comfortable viewing anytime, anywhere
06 — Results & Reflection

Impact & Key Takeaways

Design Goals & Expected Outcomes

If implemented, ZazouaLaw would achieve the following objectives:

6 steps Streamlined booking flow from search to confirmation
<5 min Expected booking time vs. 3-5 days traditional process
24/7 Access eliminates business hour constraints
100% Price transparency—no hidden consultation fees

Key Takeaways

  • Cultural Design: Arabic-first design requires understanding reading patterns and cultural expectations—not just translation
  • Trust Building: Transparency, credentials, and clear pricing are critical for professional service apps
  • Mobile Accessibility: 24/7 booking transforms legal services from intimidating to approachable

What I Would Do Differently

If I had more time and resources, I would:

  • Conduct formal user interviews with Egyptian lawyers and potential clients to validate assumptions and gather real pain points
  • Run usability testing with 8-10 participants to measure actual task completion rates and booking flow efficiency
  • Research legal regulations around client-lawyer privilege, data privacy, and digital signatures in Egypt
  • Test with diverse age groups to ensure accessibility for both tech-savvy and traditional users
  • Prototype advanced features like case tracking, document management, and video consultations for a more comprehensive solution
